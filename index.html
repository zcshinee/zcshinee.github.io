Bio

I am in my final year of Ph.D. study at MIT, mentored by Prof. Peter So, with my research centered on computational optics. I am pioneering developments in computational cameras and computational lithography, aiming to create advanced imaging and optical systems that intelligently and informatively interact with the physical world. My work navigates the challenging intersection of photolithography and computational cameras, utilizing artificial intelligence, digital fabrication, and differentiable optics. I am transforming the limitations of current design and fabrication processes, making the once impossible, possible.

In addition to my thesis, I have also delved into computational microscopy, developing techniques such as patterned illumination for super-depth imaging, and synthetic aperture for high-resolution phase imaging. Prior to MIT, I worked on computational super-resolution microscopy at Zhejiang University, where I received my master's and bachelor's degree
