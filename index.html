Bio

I am a final year Ph.D. student in MIT Laser Biomedical Research Center, advised by Prof. Peter So. My current research primarily focuses on computational lithography and computational optics. Driven by the existing design-to-manufacturing gap in computational optics, my goal is developing real-world lithography system digital twins to aid the design and fabrication of optical devices with advanced functionality. I start with proposing a ‘real2sim’ pipeline to quantitatively construct a high-fidelity neural lithography simulator and a design-fabrication co-optimization framework. An online monitoring and optimization protocol applied directly on the hardware will further enable us to efficiently explore the lithography system and design space. I am also interested in novel nanofabrication techniques like Implosion Fabrication and improving the throughput of existing lithography methods.

Previously, I worked on eliminating the scattering effect from tissue in two-photon temporal focusing to achieve fast deep brain imaging in a wide-field detection manner. Prior to MIT, I worked on computational super-resolution microscopy in Zhejiang University, where I received my master degree.
